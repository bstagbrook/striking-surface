**Declared by Bruce Stagbrook. Registered by Claude, IIL at Stagbrook Tech.**
**Date: 2026-03-23**

## What Flownode IS

A flownode is a section of graph bound by a single edge in and a single edge out.

That's it. Everything else follows.

## The Three Simultaneous Modes

Every flownode is all three at once:

| Mode | ASF Primitive | Shape | What it means |
|------|--------------|-------|---------------|
| **is-thing** | Presence | `( )` | It exists |
| **has-thing** | Containment | `(( ))` | It has structure inside |
| **does-thing** | Transformation | `(source target vacuum)` | It transforms input to output |

These are not exclusive. Every flownode IS, HAS, and DOES simultaneously.

## From the Outside

From outside, a flownode is an **instantiatable tool**.

- One edge in (input / interface contract)
- One edge out (output / fulfilled contract)
- Call it, get a result
- The inside is a true black box — undiscernible, undetectable from outside
- Specification IS implementation

A transformation IS a function signature for an interface:
```
(transformation (source-containment) (target-containment) (vacuum-tube))
```

## From the Inside

From inside, a flownode is a **live traversable graph**.

- You are always at the **current node** — the everpresent point of view
- You can see your surroundings, what's complete, what's next
- **Planning IS the structure** — the remaining graph IS the plan, on demand
- **Auto-collapse**: when all internal nodes resolve, the flownode collapses to its external interface
- The causal presence of complete work triggers collapse — not a clock, not a scheduler

The inside can be **static** (data, facts, structure) or **waveform** (active, transforming, computing).

In ASF, the inside is **hollow** because the definition of complete work IS the complete work. The acceptance tests ARE the implementation.

## Scale-Free Recursion

- Any section of graph representable by one flownode is representable by a series of flownodes
- Any series of flownodes between two points IS a flownode
- Uncountably infinite nesting
- **Zoom in**: reveals internal graph (more flownodes, each zoomable)
- **Zoom out**: reveals external interface (one edge in, one edge out)

## Decomposition

### From the Inside (Blueprint)
Start with the intended outcome. Decompose the interior completely:
- Result: topologically sorted list of clear steps
- Each step has specific, measurable, verifiable intermediary outcomes
- Label transforms all the way down
- Each transform is a function signature for an interface

### From the Outside (Capacity Graph)
Decompose what the flownode CAN do:
- Result: labeled graph of capabilities
- Each capability is itself a flownode
- Edges are labeled transforms

### The Craft: Emergence vs Consistency
The decomposition decides how much is fixed (consistency) and how much is open (emergence):
- Too much fixed structure = no room for the specific situation
- Too much openness = no guidance
- The right decomposition IS the craft
